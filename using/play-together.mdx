Normally every app on Whip keeps your data private in [your own copy](/using/open-and-use-apps). Two features let you play *with* other people: **shared sessions**, where an app's owner invites friends into one live copy everyone sees, and **leaderboards**, where an app tracks high scores so you can compete. Both arrive as [notifications](/social/notifications) when someone includes you.

## Shared sessions: everyone on the same page

By default, your data in an app is yours alone. A shared session flips that on purpose: the owner of an app can invite people into a **live shared session** where everyone sees and edits the *same* data. Think a shared grocery list the whole house updates, a party scoreboard everyone can see, or a trip planner your friends build together.

<Steps>
  <Step title="The owner invites you" icon="user-plus">
    The person who owns the app sends an invite. You'll get a notification that
    someone invited you to join their session.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You accept" icon="circle-check">
    Tap the invite to join. From that moment, you're all working in the same
    shared data.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Play together" icon="users">
    Anything anyone adds or changes, everyone sees. The app also lands on your
    [Home screen](/using/home-screen-and-folders) so you can jump back in.
  </Step>
</Steps>

A few things worth knowing:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Up to 10 people per session" icon="users-round">
    A single shared session holds up to **10 people** total, including the
    owner. Plenty for a household, a friend group, or a small team.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="You're playing in their copy" icon="house">
    When you join someone's session, you're playing in *their* copy of the app —
    that's what makes the data shared. Your own separate copy of that app steps
    aside while you're in the session.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Leaving and being removed" icon="door-open">
    You can leave a session anytime. The owner can also remove someone from
    their session. Either way, the shared data stays with the session; it doesn't
    follow you out.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Note>
  Only the app's **owner** can invite people. If you want to run a shared session
  of an app you didn't make, [remix it](/discover/remix-apps) first — then you
  own your version and can invite away.
</Note>

## Leaderboards: chase the high score

Apps — especially games — can keep a **high-score leaderboard**. It tracks your **personal best**: beat your own record and it updates; a worse run leaves your best untouched.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="One board per app" icon="trophy">
    Each app has a single leaderboard. Even as the creator ships new
    [versions](/building/versions-and-history), scores roll into the same board,
    so the competition carries across updates.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Get pinged when you're beaten" icon="bell">
    Set a great score and hold the top for a while? If someone later beats your
    score, Whip sends you a notification — your cue for a rematch.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What counts as a score" icon="target">
    The app decides what "score" means — points, fastest time, longest streak —
    and whether higher or lower wins. A speed-run can rank low-to-high; an arcade
    game high-to-low.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
  Want a leaderboard in something you're building? Ask for it in chat —
  "add a high-score leaderboard" — when you [edit your
  app](/building/edit-your-app). Whip wires it up. See more of what's possible in
  [what apps can do](/building/what-apps-can-do).
</Tip>

## Common questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How many people can be in a session?" icon="users">
    Up to 10 at once, counting the owner. Invites go out as notifications.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Does joining a session share all my data?" icon="shield">
    No — only the data in *that* session's app is shared, and only with the
    people in it. Every other app you use stays [private to your own
    copy](/using/open-and-use-apps).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I compete with strangers on a leaderboard?" icon="globe">
    A leaderboard is open to everyone who plays that public app — friends and
    strangers alike. Your entry is your personal best.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
