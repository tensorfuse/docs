Whip gives you three different ways to pull an app back, and they do different things. **Unpublish** flips a public app back to private, so it leaves the feed and search right away. **Archive** just hides an app from your own Home without changing whether it's public. **Delete** removes a version — or the whole app — for good.

Here's how each one behaves so you can pick the right one.

## The three options at a glance

| Action | What it does | Public status | Your likes & comments |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Unpublish** | Flips a version back to private | Leaves feed & search immediately | Preserved, but hidden along with the app |
| **Archive** | Hides an app from your own Home | Unchanged | Untouched |
| **Delete** | Removes a version or the whole app | Removed | Removed |

## Unpublish: back to private

Unpublishing takes a public version and makes it private again. It disappears from the [feed](/discover/the-feed), from [search](/discover/search), and from the public part of your profile — right away. Any likes and comments it collected are preserved, but they're hidden while the app is private. Change your mind later and you can [publish it](/sharing/publish-your-app) again.

<Warning>
  Making a version private also switches off its [share
  links](/sharing/share-links) — anyone holding one can no longer open it.
  Re-sharing (or re-publishing) turns the same link back on.
</Warning>

## Archive: tidy up your Home

Archiving is about *your* view, not the public's. It hides an app from your [Home screen](/using/home-screen-and-folders) so your space stays tidy — without changing whether the app is public or private. Archived apps move to the **Archived** tab on your profile, and you can unarchive them from there any time to bring them back.

<Tip>
  Reach for **Archive** when a personal app has served its purpose but you're
  not ready to lose it. Reach for **Unpublish** when you specifically want
  something *off the public feed*.
</Tip>

## Delete: remove it for good

Deleting is permanent, so it's the option to use when you're truly done.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Delete a version vs. the whole app" icon="git-branch">
    Deletion removes what you choose — a single version, or the entire app.
    Because [every edit makes a new version](/building/versions-and-history),
    you can trim a version you don't want while keeping the rest of your
    history.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is deletion reversible?" icon="trash-2">
    No. Once something is deleted it's gone — unlike unpublishing or archiving,
    there's no bringing it back. When in doubt, unpublish or archive instead.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Note>
  Deleting an app is different from [deleting your whole
  account](/account/delete-your-account) — that's a separate, self-serve flow
  with a grace period to change your mind.
</Note>
