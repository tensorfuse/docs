Following, liking, and saving are the three ways you cheer on the people and apps you love on Whip. Follow a creator to keep up with what they publish, tap the heart to like an app, and save any app to keep it handy in your library. All three also send a signal that helps great work get discovered.

## Follow a creator

Following someone tells Whip you want more of what they make. You can follow from a few places:

<Steps>
  <Step title="From a profile" icon="user">
    Open any [profile](/social/profiles) and tap **Follow**. The button flips to
    **Following**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="From a feed card" icon="layout-grid">
    Many cards in [the feed](/discover/the-feed) have an inline **Follow** button
    right on the creator's name — tap it without leaving your scroll.
  </Step>
  <Step title="From suggested creators" icon="sparkles">
    When you first join and here and there in your feed, Whip shows a **Suggested
    for you** card of creators worth a look. Tap **Follow** on any of them.
  </Step>
</Steps>

To unfollow, tap **Following** again. From a profile or a follower list you'll get a quick confirmation first; from inline buttons on cards it happens right away.

<Note>
  Following changes two things. Their new publishes can send you a
  [notification](/social/notifications), so you never miss a drop, and it helps
  shape the suggestions Whip shows you. You can follow up to 5,000 people.
</Note>

## Like an app

A like is the quickest way to say "this is good." Tap the heart on any app card, or **double-tap the app's preview** for a satisfying burst of hearts. Tap the heart again to unlike.

Likes are public — anyone can open an app's Likes list to see who tapped the heart. The creator gets a notification the first time you like one of their apps.

## Save apps to your library

A **save** is your personal bookmark. When you save someone else's app, it drops onto your [Home screen](/using/home-screen-and-folders) so you can find and open it anytime — think of it as your collection of other people's apps, kept right next to the ones you built.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap the save icon" icon="bookmark">
    On a feed card or an app's detail sheet, tap the bookmark icon. In menus,
    the same action reads **Add to home**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find it on Home" icon="house">
    Saved apps live on your **Home** screen alongside your own creations. Sort
    and organize them however you like.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove a save" icon="bookmark-x">
    Tap the icon again, or choose **Unsave** / **Remove from home** from the
    menu. Removing a saved app from Home doesn't delete it — the creator's app
    stays live for everyone.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Every card shows a save count, so popular apps wear their saves proudly.

## How this helps creators get discovered

Following, liking, and saving aren't just for you — they feed [the feed's ranking](/discover/the-feed). Whip weighs engagement when it decides what to surface, and the signals aren't all equal: a **remix** counts most, a **save** counts about twice a like, a **comment** a little more than a like, and a **like** is the baseline. Recent plays add a boost too, and brand-new apps get a fair shot at the top for their first day. So every heart and save genuinely helps a creator reach more people.

<Tip>
  Loved an app enough to want your own version? [Remixing
  it](/discover/remix-apps) is the highest compliment you can pay — and the
  strongest discovery signal of all.
</Tip>

## Common questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Where do my saved apps go?" icon="house">
    Straight to your [Home screen](/using/home-screen-and-folders), mixed in
    with the apps you've built. You can drag them into folders to keep things
    tidy.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is there a Following feed?" icon="rss">
    Not a separate tab, no. Following someone can send you a notification when
    they publish, and it shapes your suggestions — but the main feed is [For
    You and Trending](/discover/the-feed).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can the creator see that I saved their app?" icon="eye">
    They see save counts and can hit save milestones, but saves and likes are
    about discovery, not about sharing your private data. Whatever you do
    *inside* an app stays in [your own copy](/using/open-and-use-apps).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
