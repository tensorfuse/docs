Whip is for adults only — you must be **18 or older** to create an account. Whip asks for your date of birth during onboarding, before you can start building. If your birthday shows you're under 18, sign-up stops there. This keeps the community aligned with a platform where AI generates content.

## How the age check works

When you first sign up, one of the early onboarding steps asks **"When is your birthday?"** You enter your date of birth and confirm you're 18 or older. That's it — no ID upload, no photo, no extra hoops.

<Info>
  Your date of birth is used to confirm you're old enough and to keep the
  community 18+. It's private account information — see [privacy and your
  data](/account/privacy-and-data) for the full list of what Whip stores.
</Info>

## If you're under 18

If the birthday you enter makes you younger than 18, Whip shows a screen letting you know it's for builders aged 18 and up, and there's no way to continue past it. There's no appeal path inside the app. It's a hard stop, not a maybe.

## Your date of birth can't be changed later

Once you set your date of birth during onboarding, it's locked in — you can't edit it afterward. This matters if you fat-finger the year.

<Warning>
  Mistyped your birthday and got locked out even though you're 18 or older?
  Don't make a second account — email
  [support@whip.run](mailto:support@whip.run) with your handle and what
  happened, and we'll sort it out.
</Warning>

## Why Whip is 18+

We'll be straight with you: Whip is built around AI that generates apps and content from open-ended prompts, and a [shared feed](/discover/the-feed) where anyone can publish. That's a genuinely adult creative space. Rather than build a watered-down version for younger users, Whip keeps a clean 18+ line. It's the honest call, and it lets the app be what it is. To keep the community healthy at that line, public content is screened and members can [report anything off](/account/report-content).
