Every time you [edit](/building/edit-your-app) or [remix](/discover/remix-apps) an app, Whip saves a new version. Versions show up as cards on your chat timeline, each with its own icon, title, and description. You can open any earlier version and — most usefully — make any one of them the live version again. That's your undo and restore, all in one.

## How versions work

As you build and refine, your chat becomes a timeline. Each completed version drops in as its own card so you can trace how your app grew, from version 1 to wherever you've taken it.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Each version is a snapshot" icon="camera">
    A version captures your whole app at that moment. Editing never overwrites an
    older version — it adds a new one alongside it.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Its own icon, title, and info" icon="tag">
    Whip refreshes the icon, title, and description for every version, so each
    card reflects what your app actually was at that point.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Browse and open any of them" icon="folder-open">
    Tap a version card on the timeline to open that version and see exactly how
    it looked and worked.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Failures don't break history" icon="shield-check">
    If a build fails, it won't damage the versions that came before it. Your
    working history stays intact.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Restore an earlier version

Made a change you don't love? You don't undo it — you make an earlier version the live one again.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the version you want" icon="search">
    Scroll your chat timeline to the version card you'd like back, and open it to
    confirm it's the one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set it as the live version" icon="rocket">
    Make that version your active one. From then on, that's the version that
    opens when you (or anyone you've shared with) launch the app.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Keep going from there" icon="arrow-right">
    You can carry on [editing](/building/edit-your-app) from the restored
    version — new edits build on top of it as fresh versions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Restoring is non-destructive. Setting an older version as live doesn't delete
  the newer ones — you can switch back and forth anytime.
</Note>

## Two different kinds of "update"

These two are often confused, so here's the difference in plain terms:

- **A new version of your app** is what you create every time you edit or remix. These are the version cards in your chat, and you control them.
- **A new version of the Whip app itself** is an update to the Whip app on your phone. When one is available, Whip shows an **"Update available"** prompt asking you to reload or update from the store. This has nothing to do with your app's versions — it only keeps the Whip app itself current.

So if you see an update prompt, that's about Whip, not about your creation.

## Common questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I undo a change I don't like?" icon="undo">
    Open the earlier version you preferred and set it as your live version.
    That's the restore — nothing gets permanently lost.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do remixes create versions too?" icon="shuffle">
    Yes. A [remix](/discover/remix-apps) starts its own version history, separate
    from the original app it came from.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="If a build fails, do I lose my old versions?" icon="triangle-alert">
    No. A [failed build](/building/build-limits-and-failed-builds) won't touch
    the versions you already have. Send another message to try again.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Why is Whip asking me to update?" icon="download">
    That's a new version of the Whip app itself, not your creation. Reload or
    update from your app store to keep Whip current.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
